Was mentoring with Black Girls Code when I met Demond Drummer
  • Introduced myself because i was interested in working with Englewood Codes
  • Continued talking about ways to work together which led to me working at CoderSpace
  • Was already mentoring in free time, why not take a full time job
  • Particularly because I felt like if I’d had mentors who understood what I was doing with the Immature website, I’d be a super awesome dev by now
  • I wanted to be that mentor for young black coders
I’m here because this is the type of thing I enjoy doing and especially gratifying to do this with all of you
  • All of you are incredibly bright and I’m amazed at your progress -- finding tools, taking code from CodePen, hacking into Slackbot
  • You all are coders
  • What’s important isn’t knowing everything but knowing how to figure things out and how to learn and teach yourself without giving up
  • That’s our goal for you at CoderSpace, not to teach you everything but to encourage you to learn on your own and help you know that you can learn on your own
  • Your clients will likely ask for something that you don’t yet know how to do; I want you to know that you can learn it and you can do it
  • You will go for jobs where you don’t yet know all of the skills that they are looking for; I want you to go for those jobs and know that you can learn those skills on your own before the interview and further improve on the job, because that is what employers will be looking for
  • If there’s something you don’t know how to do, try learning it on your own first, and if you get stuck or you’re not sure where to look, ask me for help, I love helping
  • Take advantage of your time at CoderSpace and become great coders

How do you prepare yourself for a career in tech?

  • Less wild west now
  • Everyone's aware of the benefits and the career path and everyone's trying to get in
  • Tech changes too quickly, can't tell you what to learn
  • Learn how to learn
  • Find a source of motivation - something you like, a client project
  • Network - join meetups - you'll form a network who can tell you when there are jobs, people to vouch for you and opportunities to learn
